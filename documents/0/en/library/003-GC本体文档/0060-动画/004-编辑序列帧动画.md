### Keyframe operation
Keyframe function: Specify that the transition between two keyframes will be automatic. The transition is linear by default, or you can set the transition effect to change the transition between keyframes. <br> 
The total number of frames in the animation depends on the longest keyframe in all animation layers. <br> 
The element is in a non-appearing state until the first keyframe, and in a disappearing state after its last keyframe.

<table>
 <tr>
    <td><b>Keyframe operation</b></td> <td><b>Description</b></td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
    <td>New keyframe</td>
    <td>
    Way 1: Double click the blank area without keyframe. <br>
    Way 2: Right-click on the blank area and select <u>Create Keyframe</u> from the pop-up menu.
    </td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
    <td>Move keyframe</td>
    <td>
    Left mouse button to drag and drop keyframes to move keyframes
    </td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
    <td>Copy keyframe</td>
    <td>
    Mouse: Select <u>Copy</u> from the right mouse button menu after selecting the keyframe</u> <br> 
    Keyboard: CTRL+C after selecting the keyframe
 </tr>
 <tr>
    <td>Paste keyframe</td>
    <td>
    Pasting into a blank area creates a new copy of the previously copied keyframe<br> 
    Pasting into a keyframe where it exists overwrites the properties of that keyframe<br> 
    Mouse: right mouse button menu select <u>Paste</u><br> Keyboard: CTRL+V
 </tr>
 <tr>
    <td>Delete keyframe</td>
    <td>
    Mouse: Select <u>Delete</u> from the right mouse button menu after selecting the keyframe</u><br> 
    Keyboard: CTRL+D or DELETE after selecting the keyframe
 </tr>
 <tr>
    <td>Throwing signals</td>
    <td>
    The list of signals is managed by a unified number-name, which depends on the specific project<br> 
    Mouse: select <u>Throw Signal</u> from the right mouse button menu after selecting the keyframe</u><br>
 <tr>
    <td>Set the transition effect</td>
    <td>
    Set the transition effect from the previous keyframe to this keyframe, none means linear transition<br>
 </tr>
</table>

 </tr>
